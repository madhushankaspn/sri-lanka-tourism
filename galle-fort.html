Galle Fort

Historic & Cultural Galle, Southern Province, Sri Lanka
4.6 (4,820 reviews)

About Galle Fort

Galle Fort, also known as the Dutch Fort or the "Ramparts of Galle", is a UNESCO World Heritage Site that stands as a testament to Sri Lanka's colonial history. Originally built by the Portuguese in 1588 and extensively fortified by the Dutch during the 17th century, this historic fortress is one of the best-preserved colonial fortifications in Asia.

The fort encompasses approximately 52 hectares within its massive ramparts, featuring a unique blend of European architecture and South Asian traditions. Today, it's a living heritage site where over 400 families reside within its walls, alongside boutique hotels, art galleries, antique shops, and restaurants.

Best Time to Visit

Early morning or late afternoon to avoid heat. Weekdays are less crowded than weekends.

Entrance Fee

Free entry to fort. Museum entry: $2. Guided tours: $15-25 per person.

Exploration

Best explored on foot. Allow 3-4 hours to see main attractions. Wear comfortable shoes.

Recommended Duration

Half day for fort exploration. Full day including museums and shopping.

Location & Access

Located in Galle city, 119 km south of Colombo. Accessible by train (coastal line), bus, or car.
