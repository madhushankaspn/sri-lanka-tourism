About Yala National Park

Yala National Park is the most visited and second largest national park in Sri Lanka, bordering the Indian Ocean. The park consists of five blocks, two of which are now open to the public, and also adjoining parks. The blocks have individual names such as Ruhuna National Park (block 1) and Kumana National Park or 'Yala East' for the adjoining area.

Yala is one of the 70 Important Bird Areas (IBAs) in Sri Lanka. It harbors 215 bird species including six endemic species of Sri Lanka. The number of mammals that has been recorded from the park is 44, and it has one of the highest leopard densities in the world.

Highlights

  • Highest density of leopards in the world
  • Home to elephants, sloth bears, and crocodiles
  • Over 200 species of birds
  • Beautiful coastal scenery
  • Safari tours available year-round
  • Historical sites within the park

Best Time to Visit

Early morning or late afternoon for wildlife sightings. Dry season (Feb-July) for best visibility.

Entrance Fee

Foreigners: $15 USD, SAARC nationals: $10 USD, Locals: LKR 100

Difficulty Level

Easy (safari vehicle based). Suitable for all ages and fitness levels.

Recommended Duration

Half day to full day safari. Morning (6-10 AM) and evening (3-6 PM) safaris available.

Location

Yala National Park is located in the Southern Province of Sri Lanka, approximately 300 km from Colombo.
