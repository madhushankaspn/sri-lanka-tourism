About Sigiriya Rock Fortress

Sigiriya, also known as Lion Rock, is an ancient rock fortress located in the northern Matale District near the town of Dambulla in the Central Province, Sri Lanka. The name refers to a site of historical and archaeological significance that is dominated by a massive column of rock nearly 200 meters (660 ft) high.

According to the ancient Sri Lankan chronicle the Culavamsa, this site was selected by King Kasyapa (477 – 495 CE) for his new capital. He built his palace on the top of this rock and decorated its sides with colorful frescoes. On a small plateau about halfway up the side of this rock he built a gateway in the form of an enormous lion. The name of this place is derived from this structure — Sīhāgiri, the Lion Rock.

Highlights

  • UNESCO World Heritage Site
  • Ancient frescoes dating back to 5th century
  • Mirror Wall with ancient graffiti
  • Water gardens and sophisticated hydraulic systems
  • Breathtaking 360-degree views from the summit
  • Archaeological museum at the site

Best Time to Visit

Early morning (6:00 AM - 10:00 AM) to avoid heat and crowds. The site opens at 7:00 AM.

Entrance Fee

Foreigners: $30 USD, SAARC nationals: $15 USD, Locals: LKR 50

Difficulty Level

Moderate. Approximately 1200 steps to the summit. Not recommended for those with mobility issues.

Recommended Duration

3-4 hours to fully explore the site including gardens, frescoes, and summit.

Location

Sigiriya is located in the North Central Province of Sri Lanka, approximately 170 km from Colombo.
