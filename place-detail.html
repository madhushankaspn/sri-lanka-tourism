Sigiriya Rock Fortress

Ancient Wonder & UNESCO Site Matale District, Central Province, Sri Lanka
4.9 (6,450 reviews)

The Eighth Wonder of the World

Sigiriya, also known as Lion Rock, is an ancient rock fortress and palace ruin located in the central Matale District of Sri Lanka. Built by King Kasyapa I in the 5th century AD, this UNESCO World Heritage Site is one of the best-preserved examples of ancient urban planning and engineering.

The fortress complex includes the remains of a ruined palace, surrounded by extensive gardens, ponds, and other structures. Rising 200 meters above the surrounding plain, Sigiriya served as both a fortress and a palace, with its summit covering an area of about 1.6 hectares. The site is famous for its ancient frescoes, mirror wall with ancient graffiti, and the lion's paw entrance.

Key Attractions

  • Water Gardens: Sophisticated ancient hydraulic system
  • Frescoes: 1500-year-old paintings of celestial maidens
  • Mirror Wall: Polished wall with ancient graffiti (6th-14th century)
  • Lion's Paw Entrance: Massive lion-shaped gateway
  • Summit Palace: Remains of royal palace with panoramic views
  • Boulder Gardens: Natural rock formations and monastic caves

Climbing Sigiriya

Total Steps

Approximately 1,200 steps from base to summit

Climbing Time

1.5-2 hours up, 1 hour down (at moderate pace)

Difficulty

Moderate to challenging. Steep metal staircases near top.

Climbing Tips

  • Start early (6:30 AM) to avoid heat and crowds
  • Carry sufficient water (1-2 liters per person)
  • Wear comfortable shoes with good grip
  • Take breaks at frescoes and lion's paw
  • Not recommended for those with fear of heights or heart conditions

Sri Lanka's Cultural Triangle

Explore Ancient Kingdoms

Sigiriya is part of Sri Lanka's famous Cultural Triangle, which includes three ancient capitals:

  • Anuradhapura - First ancient capital (4th century BCE)
  • Polonnaruwa - Second ancient capital (11th century CE)
  • Kandy - Last royal capital (15th-19th century)
  • Sigiriya - Rock fortress palace (5th century CE)
  • Dambulla - Cave temple complex (1st century BCE)

Most visitors spend 3-4 days exploring the Cultural Triangle, with Sigiriya as the highlight.
