About Polonnaruwa

Polonnaruwa is the second ancient capital of Sri Lanka, declared a World Heritage Site by UNESCO in 1982. It served as the capital from the 11th to the 13th century AD, following the destruction of Anuradhapura. The ancient city is renowned for its well-preserved ruins, magnificent irrigation systems, and impressive Buddhist architecture.

The city reached its zenith under King Parakramabahu I (1153-1186 AD), who constructed magnificent palaces, temples, and the vast Parakrama Samudra (Sea of Parakrama) â€“ an enormous irrigation reservoir that remains functional today. The archaeological remains at Polonnaruwa provide a fascinating glimpse into the advanced engineering and artistic achievements of medieval Sri Lanka.

Key Attractions

  • Gal Vihara - Four magnificent Buddha statues carved from a single granite rock
  • Royal Palace of King Parakramabahu I
  • Vatadage - Circular relic house with exquisite stone carvings
  • Parakrama Samudra - Massive ancient reservoir (2,400 hectares)
  • Lankatilaka Temple - Impressive brick structure with 17m high walls
  • Archaeological Museum showcasing Polonnaruwa's history

Best Time to Visit

November to April (dry season). Morning visits are recommended to avoid midday heat.

Entrance Fee

Foreign adults: $25. SAARC countries: $15. Children: half price. Includes museum entry.

Activities

Archaeological exploration, cycling tours, photography, bird watching at the reservoir.

Recommended Duration

Full day to explore the main sites. 2 days for comprehensive exploration.

Location

Polonnaruwa is located 216 km northeast of Colombo in the North Central Province of Sri Lanka.
