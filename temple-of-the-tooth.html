Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic

Sacred & Religious Kandy, Sri Lanka
4.8 (4,215 reviews)

About the Sacred Temple

The Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic (Sri Dalada Maligawa) is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Kandy, Sri Lanka. It is one of the most sacred Buddhist temples in the world, housing the relic of the tooth of Lord Buddha. The temple was built within the royal palace complex which houses the relic, and has been a place of pilgrimage for Buddhists for centuries.

The temple complex includes the main shrine, the Royal Palace, the Audience Hall, and the Temple of the Tooth Museum. The architecture is a unique blend of Kandyan and colonial styles, with intricate wood carvings, ivory decorations, and golden roofs. The temple plays a central role in the annual Esala Perahera, one of Asia's most spectacular religious processions.

Key Features

  • Houses the Sacred Tooth Relic of Lord Buddha
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1988
  • Witness daily Puja ceremonies (offering rituals)
  • Exquisite Kandyan architecture and wood carvings
  • Temple museum with royal artifacts
  • Central venue for the Kandy Esala Perahera festival

Location

The temple is located in the heart of Kandy city, next to Kandy Lake and opposite the Royal Palace Park.

Kandy Esala Perahera Festival

Asia's Most Spectacular Festival

The Kandy Esala Perahera is an annual Buddhist festival held in July/August in honor of the Sacred Tooth Relic. This magnificent procession features:

  • Decorated Elephants: Over 100 beautifully adorned elephants
  • Traditional Dancers: Kandyan dancers, fire dancers, and acrobats
  • Drummers: Hundreds of traditional drummers and musicians
  • Fire Performances: Breathtaking fire displays and torch bearers

The festival lasts for 10 nights, culminating in the Randoli Perahera on the final night. It's considered one of Asia's most spectacular cultural events.
