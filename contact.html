Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions about traveling in Sri Lanka

What is the best time to visit Sri Lanka?

Sri Lanka has two main monsoon seasons affecting different parts of the island at different times. The west and south coasts are best from December to March, while the east coast is ideal from April to September. The hill country can be visited year-round, though it's cooler from December to February.

Do I need a visa to visit Sri Lanka?

Yes, most foreign nationals require a visa to enter Sri Lanka. You can apply for an Electronic Travel Authorization (ETA) online before your trip. The standard tourist visa allows a stay of up to 30 days and can be extended if needed.

What is the local currency and can I use credit cards?

The local currency is the Sri Lankan Rupee (LKR). Credit cards are widely accepted in hotels, restaurants, and larger shops in cities and tourist areas. However, it's advisable to carry cash for small purchases, local markets, and in rural areas.

Is Sri Lanka safe for tourists?

Sri Lanka is generally very safe for tourists. The local people are known for their hospitality and friendliness towards visitors. As with any travel destination, it's wise to take standard precautions with your belongings and avoid isolated areas at night.

What should I pack for a trip to Sri Lanka?

Lightweight, breathable clothing is recommended due to the tropical climate. Don't forget sunscreen, insect repellent, a hat, and comfortable walking shoes. If visiting temples, pack modest clothing that covers shoulders and knees. A light jacket is useful for cooler evenings in the hill country.
