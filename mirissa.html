About Mirissa Beach

Mirissa is a small town on the south coast of Sri Lanka, located in the Matara District of the Southern Province. It is approximately 150 kilometers (93 mi) south of Colombo and is situated at an elevation of 4 meters (13 ft) above sea level. Mirissa's beach and nightlife make it a popular tourist destination.

It is also a fishing port and one of the island's main whale and dolphin watching locations. The whales pass Mirissa from November to April. In 1980, a "whale watching" camp was established in Mirissa, making it one of the best places in the world to see blue whales and sperm whales.

Highlights

  • World-class whale watching (Nov-Apr)
  • Beautiful sunset views over the Indian Ocean
  • Surfing and water sports activities
  • Fresh seafood restaurants along the beach
  • Palm-fringed sandy beaches
  • Vibrant nightlife and beach parties

Best Time to Visit

November to April for whale watching. Year-round for beach activities, but avoid monsoon (May-Oct).

Entrance Fee

Free (activities charged separately). Whale watching: $40-60. Surfing lessons: $20-30.

Activities

Whale watching, surfing, snorkeling, swimming, beach volleyball, yoga classes.

Recommended Duration

2-3 days to enjoy beach activities and whale watching tours.

Location

Mirissa Beach is located in the Southern Province of Sri Lanka, approximately 150 km south of Colombo.
